How to get your feet wet with Journalism

  1. HAVE AN INTEREST:

    The first step in pursuing journalism is having a pre-existing interest in the field or a desire to pursue information.There's a lot of misunderstanding about what Journalism entails and there can be no moving forward unless you have an open mind.

  2. SHAKE OFF YOUR SUSPICIONS:

    Accepting that honesty is still very much a vital part of Journalism, it might be necessary to "detox" from inflammatory sources of media that are often presented with a partisan lean.

    Mainstream media is not "evil" or "corruptive" but it might be better to go about things a different way.

  3. SEEK OUT THE MOST PARTIAL NEWS SOURCE:

    Most communities have their own form of news coverage. Town/University papers, local news stations etc. For any fledging journalist, these will be the reality and they are invaluable resources to seeing how it's done! It also helps you to get more active in your ccommunity. If you support them, they will support you.

  4. RESEARCH EVERYTHING:

    That's not just in reference to the aformentioned news source, you as an individual should get used to looking into anything and everything. As with any skill, immersion is the best way to get a grasp of what real journalism is. I encourage you to go down rabbit-holes about things you're interested in. Try reading articles and following the citations that are listed within to see how the author obtained their info.

  5. DON'T BE AFRAID

    Discovering a new interest or buckling down on a new one can be scary. There's no harm done in trying to get more educated about how information is communicated. You come face to face with Journalism all day, every day. It's nothing to be scared of! Even if you don't plan to adjust the focus of your life into pursuing it as a career, developing a finer eye for the way you gather information is a valuable skill.
